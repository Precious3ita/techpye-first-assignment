In martial arts, different angles are used for different movements. For example, a spinning kick uses a 360-degree angle, while kicks with multiple spins may use more than 360 degrees.

  • 45 Degree Kick: is a fundamental kick in Taekwondo and is also known as a diagonal kick or the lower version of a roundhouse kick.
  • Front Kick: is a martial arts technique that involves lifting the knee and then straightening the leg to strike a target.
  • Side Kick: is a martial arts move that involves raising the knee, rotating the body, and extending the leg to strike an opponent.
  • In-out Kick:also known as the inside-outside crescent kick and outside-inside crescent kick, is a martial arts kick that involves swinging the knee of the kicking leg in a circular motion.
  • Stretch Kick: is a low-impact technique in martial arts that helps improve flexibility and kicking techniques.
  • Out-in Kick:In Taekwondo, an out-in kick is a crescent kick that's performed in two directions: outward and inward
    1. Outward crescent kick: The practitioner raises their extended leg as high as possible, slightly across the body's centerline, and then sweeps outward in a circular arc.
    2. Inward crescent kick: The practitioner performs the same motions as the outward kick, but the direction of the kick is reversed, originating from the outside of the body and heading towards the inside.
